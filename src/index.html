How To Use
  1. Enter your ride height. If your car is not lowered, set it to 0.00 in. If you're lowered 1", set it to -1.00 in. As the car is lowered, the wheels will naturally be pushed outwards, so the amount of offset needed for "perfect flushness" changes.
  2. Adjust the width and offset fields to match whatever the width and offset of your wheels are.
  3. Ideally, your plot will fall somewhere in between "Perfect Offset for Rear Flushness" and "Perfect Offset for Front Flushness". The front and rears require different offsets, so try and make whatever compromises you have to.
  4. Each of the sliders will snap to increments that make sense. You can turn that off by unchecking the SNAP button. You can also lock a value by checking the LOCK button.
  5. You'll also notice dots plotted on the graph. The dots represent setups from other members of the community for you to reference. You can click on one of them to show information in the bottom of the screen.
